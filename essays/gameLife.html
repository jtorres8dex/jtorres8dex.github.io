The Gameification of Life

RLLib and its Implications

Spending a good amount of time developing AI agents to perform in simulated systems, I have had the realization that this framework also describes a useful model of life [1]. Viewing things as if we are agents playing a game trying to maximize some reward has great utility in my opinion. We have desires (some state we are trying to get to) and must learn the rules of the game (understand both the situation we are in) and act in ways that will get us what we want(reason how we can manipulate inputs to it to get a desired output)

Skill Issues

There are few things you cannot improve by actively trying to in a clever way. So many of your insecurities are a skill issue. You can educate yourself, improve physical appearance, become more sociable, strengthen your professional & social network, mature emotionally, .

Genetics and circumstance of birth of course play a huge roll in these things. You don't get to choose where you fall on the bell curve of any given characteristic, at least for your initial conditions. But consistent, calculated actions for a long enough time, without pompously convincing yourself you're more talented than you are or pesimistically convincing yourself you're dumber than you are, can always monotonically improve your situation.

The Games We Play

Given that everyone has their own internal model of the world, everyone is going to have different incentives. Different incentives means playing different games. In other words, everyone is trying to get something different out of life. The most obvious game people play is trying to work their way up in their career. But alot of people are simply trying to maximize sources of pleasure in their life. Their job is just something they do to be able to live. An easy example of this population can be found among people around my age - in their early 20's. They prioritize traveling, dating, and living an aesthetical lifestyle. I believe these principles still apply. You can iteratively improve at all these things. And I dont judge, these games are just as arbitrary as the ones I choose to play.

Its not a novel idea that different people play different games. I think the more interesting question is why people choose the games they play. Why do some people obsess over professional status whereas others are motivated by maximizing a source of pleasure? An easy starting point is to consider two different people. One is a conventionally attractive woman with an IQ of 100 and the other is a conventionally unattractive male with an IQ of 130. From an early age, these two people will be handed hugely different reward functions. Its easy to predict the path that each are likely to follow. We tend to repeatedly push our chips, and take an interest along the way, into things we have a natural affinity towards.
