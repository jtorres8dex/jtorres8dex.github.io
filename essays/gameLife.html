The Gameification of Life

RLLib and its Implications

Spending a good amount of time developing AI agents to perform in simulated systems, I have had the realization that this framework also describes a useful model of life [1]. Viewing things as if we are agents playing a game trying to maximize some reward has great utility in my opinion. We have desires (some state we are trying to get to) and must learn the rules of the game (understand both the situation we are in) and act in ways that will get us what we want(reason how we can manipulate inputs to it to get a desired output)

Skill Issues

There are few things you cannot improve by actively trying to in a clever way. So many of your insecurities are a skill issue. You can educate yourself, improve physical appearance, become more sociable, strengthen your professional & social network, mature emotionally, .

Genetics and circumstance of birth of course play a huge roll in these things. You don't get to choose where you fall on the bell curve of any given characteristic. But consistent, calculated actions for a long enough time, without pompously convincing yourself you're more talented than you are or pesimistically convincing yourself you're dumber than you are, can always monotonically improve your situation.
