I want to one day start a company. I've had various experiences that led me to this conclusion but just assume I know with absolute certainty that this is what I want to do. Given this vague desire

NOTE: I have zero formal education in business or economics. I am very likely getting going to say something that is debatable at the very least. This is moreso an explanation on what my mental model currently is.

Coming out of undergrad I wholeheartedly believed that the best way to build a foundation to build a company was to work for a startup. I still believe this to be a very valid argument and true in many cases. However, my technical experience (and really the things I enjoy working on the most) are primarily Robotics & Aerospace. Starting a company in these industries is a whole other beast. You're not making some SAAS product and marketing your way into a positive cash flow. You have to build something that is incredibly difficult and expensive. There are almost no flukes in Deep Tech. I am confident that I am intelligent enough to work at one of these startups, but founding one is likely not the best strategy for me. Put simply, I have the intellectual self awareness that Deep Tech is not my best bet given my skill set.

It follows then, that my best bet at starting a company is one that is not as technical as an AI robotics company but still leveraging some skill I have. Still, these definitions are to vague.
